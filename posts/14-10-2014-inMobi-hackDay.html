Taala and InMobi HackDay 2014

Office? Good. Hack? Good. Learning Experience? Brilliant.

Posted by Ravi Tejasvi on October 14, 2014 Picture source: PravChuk's Lumia

Inception of the idea.

My friend Praveen Chukka had got to know of the hackathon in the first place. He asked me to join him and I said yes. :'). We initially thought of an application which aided the memory by giving awesome patterns and nemonics to remember numbers and everything nice.
( source : giphy.com )
But we soon realised that the impact this would create wouldn't be that great.
So we sat there in the hackathon without an idea. Thinking of all the "impactful" billion dollar ideas which we could convert into a prototype within 18 hours.
After thinking for a while, we came up with a simple idea.
An extended child lock. For your friends. :P We all have appLockers. And they do provide all them good features, but they aren't the one tap simple solution. Instead they make us ourselves unlock an app 40 to 50 times a day, when the sole purpose of the app was to prevent others from opening the "sensitive" data.

What it did

So basically it was a profile setter for your phone, if you had it you would set it to root mode, giving you all the access. But if you were giving to your friends, you would change it to the friends profile by one tap on your notification bar or any other custom profile which the app lets you create.

For each profile you can choose which all apllication to be blocked.

How we did it

So we started by trying to understand how to get to know when an application is launched. This logically should be our starting point. Once we get to know what application is being launched we can block it if it's in the list.

    How to get this information :
  • Read logs.
  • Broadcast Recievers

Reading locks was disabled from Jellybean. You Jelly?
Other apps don't get any broadcast when an app is started, so that's out of question.
So we had to implement a listener which kept checking what activity was on top of stack.
Then we used packageManager to get all details of this activity and checked it against the blocked list. If anything matched, we showed a blocked page.

Final presentation round

Our final presentation was in front of an elite panel. This consisted of Ravi Gururaj, no introduction needed here, Mr Manu Jain, country head for Xiaomi, Mr Namit Jain, director at Nutanix, and inMobi head of products Krish.
I feel that the panel was more into checking whether the hack we made was some disruptive business.
This should be one way of looking at it but I think the more important thing was to see the hack. Not the business side of it. Only if you create products for fun which simplifies life without looking whether it will be the next facebook or google can you actually build something really disruptive.
Having said that, I feel our presentation wasn't that good either, we didn't make it clear how much and how well we had hacked.
All in all, it was a good day. Learnt so much and met amazing bunch of people. ( source : giphy.com )
