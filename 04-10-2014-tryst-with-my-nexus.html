Time when me and my nexus almost broke up.

"She never listened, just kept repeating the same thing." Boot loop.

Posted by Owner of that nexus on October 04, 2014 Picture source: cnet.com

This is one long story. tl;dr; jump to the solution.

5 minutes before the tragedy

One fine day, I was texting my friend on the FB messenger app. I keep my dear nexus on the bed. And go to freshen up. I come back and see it's switched off ( play dramatic music. ). Has it ever happened to you that you leave her for 5 mins and she has just turned off? ;)

Research

I didn't know what to do. I pressed the power button. There came the google logo. And then again. And again. Aaaaand again.
Kids. This phenomena is called boot loop, may also be a soft brick.

After googling it for possible solutions and from the advice of my awesome friends on facebook, I found that either my phone was bricked for which I needed to go to recovery mode and do some changes or the power button was screwed up.

Flashback!

Now some quick background on my dear nexus.
It was bought in the states via play store. Hence warranty was not applicable here, in India, even though I was well within the warranty period.
Sending it to States would prove costly, but this was the last resort. The phone was rooted. Hence in the bootloader menu it showed that it was unlocked. -_- Why you little.

Being Sherlock

I tried going to the bootloader to clear dalvik cache just in case it was a soft brick. But in the menu before I could go to the recovery mode, it used to automatically select (power button is used for selecting.) whatever was on screen within a second. This lead me to think it may have been a faulty power button. Elementry, my dear Watson. ( source : giphy.com )
It's here where I decided to go to the LG service center.

Visiting LG 'self' service center.

So I found this big LG service center. This rekindled the lost spirit in me. Showed him the problem. And before I finished telling him everything he cuts me and says, "Nothing I can do Saar. It's yemerican model. I can change the motherboard which will cost you 12 grand rupees. Or you send this there." I rebeled, "Listen to the problem, it might be a faulty power button". He was quite a rebel too, "Eh no man. My brother in law also had same problem, he also asked me to do something. I sent him off to U.S.A."
Now thoroughly confused whether to send my phone or myself to the states I went to a local shop who said he was busy day and night as his marriage was around the corner. I didn't even want so much detail. I went to another LG 'do it yourself' service center. Who kept my phone for a week and returned it saying the same thing. "Motherboard gone Sir. Replace and give me 12k cash." ( source : giphy.com ) Exactly Ryan. Exactly.

The Temptation

This is where I feel a little guilty with myself. I for a second thought of paying 12k and not work more to solve a problem at hand. And when I realised it only gave me 3 months warranty, I set my eyes on Moto X for a while. But then something happened. My Nexus 5 got jealous.

Haaallelujah!

Sitting in Krespy kreme, I was requested to display my friends the demo of my faulty nexus. Turning phone away from me I switched it on. Counted till three and said "and now it restarts."
Only it didn't. I had pressed the area behind the camera and the power button to hear a click previously. It was working somehow.

I ran to the car, got my mac. Recovered important data, cleared dalvik cache, did a factory reset. Downloaded stock image and unrooted the phone. Updated it to 4.4.4. And now it works fine.

So the culprit? Mostly the faulty power button. Give your nexus a tight slap if it misbehaves and doesn't let you choose something from the bootloader menu. That should do. Also add "Somebody gonna get a hurt real bad" to give some style and scare your phone into working.
